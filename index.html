Meditation #4

by Maceo

For this meditation, I was initially stumped because creating a new tuning system that actually sounded good seemed very difficult when I was experimenting. Eventually, I started thinking about tuning systems in my own life and realized I could draw inspiration from the guitar tuners I use. Thatâ€™s what I decided to recreate for my meditation.

I started by looking up the frequeuncies for each open string in standard tuning on the guitar and made a list. From there, I created play functions and then made functions to both increase or decrease the strings by one semitone. This project also ended up being practical as I tested the frequencies against my actual guitar in standard tuning and drop D (only low e string) tuning and they were perfectly in tune. Having my guitar and computer harmonize also made it very easy to hear their individual timbres. Another thing I enjoyed playing around with was tuning down 20 semitones and using the E, A, and D "strings" to create bass melodies.
